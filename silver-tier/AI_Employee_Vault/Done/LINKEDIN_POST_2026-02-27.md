## Draft Post

AI isn't coming for your job — but someone using AI might be.

That's not a threat. It's a reality check.

We're living through the most significant shift in how work gets done since the industrial revolution. AI tools are compressing hours into minutes, automating the repetitive, and freeing up human energy for what actually matters — strategy, creativity, and connection.

Here's what I've learned:

→ AI amplifies good thinking. It doesn't replace it.
→ The people winning right now aren't the ones afraid of AI — they're the ones experimenting with it daily.
→ The skill of the decade isn't coding or prompting. It's knowing WHAT to ask and WHY.

The professionals who will thrive aren't those who resist the wave — they're the ones who learn to surf it.

Where are you on that spectrum? Still watching from the shore, or already in the water?
